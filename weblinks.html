useful links

1. Khan Academy (khanacademy.org): A cornerstone of my educational resources, Khan Academy offers comprehensive lessons on a wide array of subjects. From mathematics to science, history, and computing, it's an excellent platform for brushing up on old skills or learning something new entirely.

2. Medium (medium.com): Medium is my go-to for thought-provoking articles, personal essays, and insightful commentary on a broad spectrum of topics, including technology, culture, and self-improvement. It's a platform where independent voices share their perspectives and expertise.

3. Canva (canva.com): For all my design needs, Canva is a lifesaver. It simplifies the design process with its user-friendly interface and vast library of templates, making professional-looking graphics accessible to everyone, regardless of their design skills.

4. Trello (trello.com): Trello keeps my life organized. It's a flexible project management tool that helps me track my tasks, projects, and goals using boards, lists, and cards. It's incredibly versatile, useful for everything from planning a trip to managing large work projects.

5. Coursera (coursera.org): Coursera has been instrumental in my professional development, offering courses from universities and companies around the world. Whether it's learning about artificial intelligence, marketing, or psychology, Coursera provides a wealth of knowledge at my fingertips.

6. Pocket (getpocket.com): Pocket is my digital library for content I want to read later. It allows me to save articles, videos, and stories from any publication, page, or app, curating my own feed of interesting reads that I can access even offline.

7. Duolingo (duolingo.com): Learning languages is a passion of mine, and Duolingo makes it fun and engaging. With its gamified approach, I'm able to practice reading, writing, and speaking in several languages, making progress in daily bite-sized lessons.
